About Asbestos Exposure Compensation

Victims of asbestos exposure may be entitled to compensation from:

  • Asbestos Trust Funds: Over $30 billion set aside by bankrupt asbestos companies
  • Lawsuits Against Manufacturers: Companies that produced or used asbestos products
  • Workers' Compensation: For occupational asbestos exposure
  • VA Benefits: For veterans exposed to asbestos during military service

Types of Damages in Asbestos Claims

  • Economic Damages: Medical bills, lost income, future treatment costs, lost earning capacity
  • Non-Economic Damages: Pain and suffering, emotional distress, loss of quality of life
  • Punitive Damages: Awarded in cases of gross negligence or willful misconduct by defendants
  • Wrongful Death: Additional compensation for surviving family members

Factors That Affect Compensation

  • Type of Diagnosis: Mesothelioma cases typically receive higher settlements ($1M-$2.4M average)
  • Disease Stage: More advanced illness = higher damages due to reduced life expectancy
  • Exposure Duration: Longer exposure = stronger causation link = higher compensation
  • Number of Defendants: More responsible parties = higher total recovery potential
  • State Law: Caps on non-economic damages vary by state
  • Age & Dependents: Younger victims with families typically receive higher awards

Important Notes

  • This calculator provides estimates only. Actual compensation varies widely based on specific case facts.
  • Average mesothelioma settlements range from $1 million to $2.4 million.
  • Statute of limitations for asbestos claims varies by state (typically 1-6 years from diagnosis).
  • Most asbestos cases settle out of court. Trials can result in higher awards but take longer.
  • Multiple sources of compensation can be pursued simultaneously (trust funds + lawsuits + VA benefits).
  • Consult an experienced asbestos attorney for a case evaluation.
