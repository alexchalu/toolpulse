Understanding Crypto Market Cap

Market capitalization is the total value of a cryptocurrency, calculated by multiplying the current price by the circulating supply. It's one of the most important metrics for evaluating and comparing cryptocurrencies.

📊 Why Market Cap Matters

  • Valuation Metric: Helps compare different cryptocurrencies fairly
  • Risk Assessment: Generally, higher market cap = lower volatility risk
  • Investment Strategy: Different strategies for large-cap vs small-cap coins
  • Market Position: Shows where a coin stands in the overall crypto ecosystem

🎯 Market Cap Categories

  • Large-cap (>$10B): Bitcoin, Ethereum — established, lower risk
  • Mid-cap ($1B-$10B): Growing projects — moderate risk/reward
  • Small-cap (<$1B): Emerging projects — high risk, high potential return

💡 Coin Dominance

Dominance measures a coin's market share compared to the total crypto market. Bitcoin dominance above 50% typically indicates market uncertainty, while declining dominance often signals an "altcoin season."

📈 Using This Calculator

Target Price: Calculate what price a coin needs to reach a specific market cap.

⚠️ Important Considerations

  • Circulating vs Total Supply: Only use circulating supply for accurate market cap
  • Dilution Risk: Check if more coins will be released (token vesting schedules)
  • Market Manipulation: Small-cap coins are more vulnerable to pump-and-dump schemes
  • Not Investment Advice: Market cap is one metric among many — do comprehensive research

🔍 Advanced Analysis Tips

  • Compare market cap to trading volume for liquidity assessment
  • Track fully diluted valuation (FDV) for tokens with locked supply
  • Monitor market cap changes relative to Bitcoin dominance
  • Consider market cap in relation to real-world adoption metrics
