New Jersey Car Accident Settlement Laws

New Jersey follows modified comparative (50%) rules for personal injury claims. The statute of limitations for filing a car accident lawsuit in NJ is 2 years from the date of the accident.

How New Jersey Fault Rules Affect Your Settlement

Under New Jersey's modified comparative (50%) system, your compensation may be reduced or barred based on your percentage of fault in the accident.

You can recover damages as long as your fault does not exceed the threshold. If it does, you receive nothing.

Average Car Accident Settlements in New Jersey

  • Minor injuries (whiplash, sprains): $5,000 – $25,000
  • Moderate injuries (fractures, disc herniation): $25,000 – $100,000
  • Serious injuries (surgery, long recovery): $100,000 – $500,000
  • Severe/catastrophic (TBI, spinal cord): $500,000 – $5,000,000+

What Affects Your NJ Settlement Value?

  • Total medical expenses (past and future)
  • Lost wages and diminished earning capacity
  • Pain and suffering multiplier (typically 1.5x to 5x medical costs)
  • Your percentage of fault under New Jersey's modified comparative (50%) rules
  • Insurance policy limits of the at-fault driver
  • Quality of evidence (photos, police report, witnesses)
  • Whether you hired an attorney

Steps After a Car Accident in New Jersey

  • Call 911 and get a police report filed
  • Seek immediate medical attention (even if injuries seem minor)
  • Document everything: photos, witness info, medical records
  • Do NOT give a recorded statement to the other driver's insurance
  • Consult a New Jersey personal injury attorney within the 2 years statute of limitations
  • Keep all receipts for medical bills, prescriptions, and lost work
