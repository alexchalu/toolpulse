Understanding Mesothelioma Compensation

What Damages Are Included?

  • Economic Damages: Medical expenses (surgery, chemotherapy, radiation, experimental treatments), lost wages, lost earning capacity, travel costs for treatment
  • Non-Economic Damages: Pain and suffering, emotional distress, loss of quality of life, loss of consortium (family relationships)
  • Punitive Damages: In cases of gross negligence or intentional harm by defendants (varies by state)

Average Mesothelioma Settlements & Verdicts

  • Settlement Range: $1 million - $1.4 million (average)
  • Trial Verdict Range: $2.4 million (median) to $10+ million (though verdicts can be reduced on appeal)
  • VA Claims: $2,000 - $4,000/month in disability benefits
  • Trust Fund Claims: $200,000 - $400,000 (from asbestos bankruptcy trusts)

Factors That Affect Compensation

  • Disease Severity: Advanced stage cases typically receive higher compensation
  • Age: Younger patients may receive more for lost earning years
  • Exposure History: Clear documentation of asbestos exposure strengthens cases
  • Defendant Solvency: Bankrupt companies may only pay from trust funds (limited amounts)
  • State Laws: Caps on non-economic damages vary by jurisdiction
  • Quality of Legal Team: Experienced mesothelioma lawyers achieve higher settlements

Timeline for Mesothelioma Claims

  • Statute of Limitations: 1-3 years from diagnosis (varies by state) - DO NOT DELAY
  • Case Investigation: 2-4 months to gather medical records and exposure evidence
  • Filing to Settlement: 6-18 months for most cases
  • Trial Cases: 1-3 years (if settlement not reached)
  • Expedited Cases: Many courts offer fast-track for terminally ill plaintiffs

Types of Compensation Sources

  • Personal Injury Lawsuit: Filed while patient is alive
  • Wrongful Death Lawsuit: Filed by family after death
  • Asbestos Trust Funds: From bankrupt asbestos companies (60+ trusts with $30+ billion)
  • VA Benefits: For veterans exposed during military service
  • Workers' Compensation: May apply for occupational exposure

How This Calculator Works

The calculator uses industry data and legal precedents to estimate compensation:

  • Economic Damages: Medical costs + (Annual income × Years lost working)
  • Pain & Suffering Multiplier: 2-5× economic damages based on severity, age, and stage
  • Exposure Adjustment: Longer exposure periods and multiple defendants increase settlement leverage
  • Range: Conservative estimate (settlement) to optimistic (jury verdict)

Important Considerations

  • No Upfront Costs: Mesothelioma attorneys work on contingency (typically 25-40% of recovery)
  • Multiple Claims: You can file against multiple defendants and access trust funds simultaneously
  • Tax-Free: Compensation for physical injury/illness is generally not taxable
  • Family Claims: Spouses and children may have separate claims for loss of consortium
  • Veterans: Can pursue both VA benefits AND civil lawsuits

Who Is Eligible?

  • Anyone diagnosed with mesothelioma caused by asbestos exposure
  • Family members exposed to asbestos from a loved one's work clothes (secondary exposure)
  • Residents near asbestos mines or contaminated sites
  • Navy veterans, shipyard workers, construction workers, auto mechanics
  • Surviving family members (wrongful death claims)
