- I take 40%
- Fair split for shared effort

- Good if I'm heavily involved in sales/onboarding

## Sales Call Script

### Opening:
> Thanks for hopping on. I know your time is valuable, so I'll keep this tight.
> 
> The big idea: Most web agencies leave money on the table because clients want MORE than just a pretty website, but you don't have the dev resources to build complex automation.
> 
> I'm proposing we partner so you can say YES to those requests without hiring.

### Discovery Questions:
1. How often do clients ask for chatbots, automation, or booking systems?
2. What do you typically tell them when they ask?
3. How many active clients do you have right now?
4. What's your average monthly retainer per client?
5. Have you ever tried to white-label dev services before?

### Value Proposition:
> Here's what this looks like in practice:
> 
> - You introduce AI automation as an add-on to existing clients
> - Typical pricing: $2,500-3,500 setup + $300-500/mo
> - You keep 60%, I keep 40%
> - With just 5 clients, you're adding $15K/year recurring
> - I handle ALL technical work, you stay the face of the relationship

### Objection Handling:

**"My clients can't afford that."**
> If they're paying you $1K+/month for web + marketing, they can afford $400/mo for automation. The ROI is clear — most businesses see 5-10x return in captured leads alone.

**"I don't want to complicate my service offerings."**
> Totally fair. This isn't for every agency. But if you have clients asking for this stuff, you're either saying no (and losing potential revenue) or referring them elsewhere (and losing the relationship). This keeps it in-house.

**"How do I know my clients will actually use it?"**
> Great question. We start with a free audit/demo for your client. They see the value BEFORE they commit. If they're not excited after the demo, we don't move forward.

### Close:
> So here's what I'm thinking: Let's do a pilot with one of your clients.
> 
> You pick a client who's been asking for automation or who has a clear lead capture problem. I'll build them a custom demo (free). We show it to them. If they love it, we move forward. If not, no harm done.
> 
> That way you can see the process, the quality of work, and the client reaction — all without any financial risk.
> 
> Sound fair?
