How Capital Gains Tax Works in Maine

When you sell an investment — stocks, bonds, cryptocurrency, real estate, or other assets — for more than you paid, the profit is a capital gain. In Maine, capital gains are generally taxed as ordinary income at rates up to 7.15%, on top of federal capital gains tax.

Federal Capital Gains Tax Rates (2026)

  • 0% rate: Single filers with taxable income up to $47,025 (MFJ: $94,050)
  • 15% rate: Single filers $47,025–$518,900 (MFJ: $94,050–$583,750)
  • 20% rate: Single filers above $518,900 (MFJ: above $583,750)
  • 3.8% NIIT: Net Investment Income Tax applies to income above $200K (single) or $250K (MFJ)

Short-Term vs. Long-Term Capital Gains

Short-term gains (assets held less than 1 year) are taxed at your ordinary income tax rate — which can be as high as 37% federally plus 7.15% in Maine. Long-term gains (assets held 1+ years) receive preferential federal rates of 0%, 15%, or 20%, but Maine does not offer a preferential state rate — gains are taxed as ordinary income.

5 Strategies to Reduce Capital Gains Tax in Maine

  • Hold investments for 1+ years — Long-term gains qualify for 0/15/20% federal rates instead of up to 37%
  • Tax-loss harvesting — Sell losing investments to offset gains (up to $3,000 excess losses deductible)
  • Use tax-advantaged accounts — 401(k), IRA, Roth IRA, HSA shield gains from taxation
  • Qualified Opportunity Zones — Defer and potentially reduce capital gains by investing in QOZ funds
  • Consider your timing — Sell in years when your income is lower to stay in a lower federal bracket

Maine-Specific Capital Gains Rules

Capital gains taxed as ordinary income at graduated rates up to 7.15%. Combined with federal rates, investors in Maine could pay up to 31.0% on long-term gains or up to 47.9% on short-term gains. Tax planning is essential for Maine residents with significant investment income.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the capital gains tax rate in Maine?
Maine taxes capital gains at rates up to 7.15%. Capital gains taxed as ordinary income at graduated rates up to 7.15%. Combined with federal tax (0-20%) and the potential 3.8% NIIT, your total rate could reach 31.0%.
How do I calculate capital gains tax on stocks in Maine?
Subtract your cost basis (purchase price + fees) from your sale price to determine your gain. If held over 1 year, apply federal long-term rates (0/15/20% based on income) plus Maine's state rate (up to 7.15%). Our calculator above handles all of this automatically.
Does Maine tax cryptocurrency capital gains?
Yes — cryptocurrency is treated as property by the IRS and by Maine. Crypto gains are taxed the same as stocks or other investments — at your ordinary income tax rate for short-term gains or preferential federal rates for long-term gains, plus the Maine state rate of up to 7.15%.
Is there a capital gains exclusion for home sales in Maine?
Yes — the federal home sale exclusion allows you to exclude up to $250,000 in gains ($500,000 for married couples filing jointly) if you've lived in the home for at least 2 of the last 5 years. This exclusion applies to both federal and Maine state taxes. Gains above the exclusion limit are taxed at your applicable rates.
What is the Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)?
The NIIT is a 3.8% federal surtax on investment income (capital gains, dividends, interest, rental income) for individuals with modified AGI above $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (married filing jointly). It applies in all states, including Maine.
How can I reduce my capital gains tax in Maine?
Key strategies include: holding investments for over 1 year (lower federal rate), tax-loss harvesting (offsetting gains with losses), using tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA, Roth), donating appreciated assets to charity (avoid gains entirely), and timing your sales to stay in lower tax brackets.
