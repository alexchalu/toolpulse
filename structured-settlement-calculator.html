❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is a structured settlement? +
A structured settlement is a financial arrangement where a claim is resolved through periodic payments over time rather than a single lump sum. These payments are typically funded through an annuity purchased by the defendant or their insurance company.
Why would I want to sell my structured settlement? +
Common reasons include needing immediate cash for medical expenses, home purchases, education costs, or other financial emergencies. Selling can provide liquidity while giving up future payments.
How is the present value calculated? +
Present value is calculated using discounted cash flow analysis. Future payments are discounted back to today's dollars using the discount rate, which accounts for the time value of money and risk.
What is a typical discount rate used by settlement buyers? +
Settlement purchasing companies typically use discount rates ranging from 8% to 15%, depending on the length of payments remaining, state regulations, and the specific terms of the settlement.
Are there any fees or taxes on the cash-out amount? +
Yes, there may be legal fees, administrative costs, and taxes. The calculator provides estimates before these deductions. Always consult with a tax professional for your specific situation.
How long does the process take to sell a structured settlement? +
The process typically takes 4-12 weeks from application to final court approval. This includes finding a buyer, completing paperwork, and obtaining court approval (required in most states).
Is selling my structured settlement legal? +
Yes, selling structured settlements is legal under federal law (Structured Settlement Protection Acts of 2001 and 2018) and requires court approval in all 50 states to protect sellers from unfair transactions.
