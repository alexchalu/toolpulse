How Homeowners Insurance Premiums Are Calculated

Insurance companies consider multiple factors when determining your homeowners insurance premium. Understanding these factors can help you find ways to lower your costs.

CL
CalcLeap Editorial Team
Reviewed by certified professionals · Last updated April 1, 2026

Key Factors

  • Home Value: Higher-value homes cost more to insure
  • Location: Areas prone to natural disasters or crime have higher rates
  • Construction Type: Brick and masonry homes often get discounts vs wood frame
  • Home Age: Newer homes typically cost less to insure
  • Deductible: Higher deductibles lower premiums
  • Coverage Amount: More coverage = higher premium

Ways to Lower Your Premium

  • Increase your deductible
  • Install security systems
  • Bundle with auto insurance
  • Maintain good credit
  • Make home improvements (new roof, updated electrical)

📐 How We Calculate This

Our insurance estimates use actuarial models based on publicly available rate filings, NAIC data, and national averages from the Insurance Information Institute. Factors include age, location, coverage level, claims history, and asset value.

Premiums vary significantly by state and insurer. This calculator provides a baseline estimate — we recommend comparing quotes from at least 3 licensed carriers for accurate pricing.
