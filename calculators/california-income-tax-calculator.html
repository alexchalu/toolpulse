About California Income Tax

California has one of the most progressive income tax systems in the United States, with nine tax brackets ranging from 1% to 13.3%. This calculator helps you estimate your California state income tax based on the 2026 tax brackets and standard deductions.

2026 California Tax Brackets

Single Filers

  • 1% on income up to $10,412
  • 2% on income $10,413 to $24,684
  • 4% on income $24,685 to $38,959
  • 6% on income $38,960 to $54,081
  • 8% on income $54,082 to $68,350
  • 9.3% on income $68,351 to $349,137
  • 10.3% on income $349,138 to $418,961
  • 11.3% on income $418,962 to $698,271
  • 12.3% on income $698,272 to $1,000,000
  • 13.3% on income over $1,000,000

  • 8% on income $108,163 to $136,700

  • 11.3% on income $837,923 to $1,396,542

  • 13.3% on income over $2,000,000

Standard Deductions (2026)

  • Single: $5,363
  • Married Filing Jointly: $10,726
  • Head of Household: $10,726

Key Features

  • Progressive tax system with 9 brackets
  • Top marginal rate of 13.3% (highest in the nation)
  • Standard deduction reduces taxable income
  • Additional 1% Mental Health Services Tax on income over $1 million
  • No local income taxes (state-level only)

How to Lower Your California Tax

  • Maximize retirement contributions: 401(k), IRA contributions reduce taxable income
  • Health Savings Account (HSA): Tax-deductible contributions
  • Itemize deductions: If total deductions exceed standard deduction
  • Tax credits: Earned Income Tax Credit, dependent care credits
  • Property tax deduction: Limited by federal SALT cap

Important Notes

This calculator provides estimates only. Actual tax liability may vary based on credits, additional deductions, and other factors. Consult a tax professional for personalized advice.

Federal income tax, Social Security, and Medicare taxes are separate and not included in this calculation. This calculator only computes California state income tax.
