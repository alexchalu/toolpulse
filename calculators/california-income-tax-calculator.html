Understanding California Income Tax

California has a progressive income tax system, meaning higher earners pay a larger percentage of their income in taxes. Tax rates and brackets are adjusted annually for inflation. Key factors influencing your tax liability include your filing status, adjusted gross income (AGI), deductions, and credits.

Filing Statuses:

  • Single: For individuals who are unmarried or legally separated.
  • Married Filing Jointly: For married couples who choose to file a single tax return together.
  • Married Filing Separately: For married couples who choose to file individual tax returns.
  • Head of Household: For unmarried individuals who pay more than half the cost of keeping up a home for themselves and a qualifying person.
  • Qualifying Widow(er): For certain widows or widowers with a dependent child.

It's always recommended to consult with a qualified tax professional for personalized tax advice or refer to the official California Franchise Tax Board (FTB) website for the most accurate and up-to-date information.
