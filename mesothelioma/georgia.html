❓ Georgia Mesothelioma FAQ

What is the average mesothelioma settlement in Georgia?

The average mesothelioma settlement in Georgia is approximately $1.2M. However, settlements can range from $100,000 to over $10 million depending on factors like exposure duration, number of defendants, severity of illness, and whether the case goes to trial. Trust fund claims typically provide additional $50,000-$400,000+ on top of lawsuit settlements.

How long do I have to file a mesothelioma lawsuit in Georgia?

Georgia has a statute of limitations of 2 years for personal injury claims. The clock typically starts when you receive your mesothelioma diagnosis. For wrongful death claims, family members generally have 2 years from the date of death. Don't delay — consult an attorney immediately after diagnosis.

Where was asbestos used in Georgia?

Georgia has 195 documented asbestos exposure sites. Major exposure occurred in Textile, Paper, Military industries. Notable sites include Kings Bay Naval Base, Plant Scherer, Savannah shipyards. Workers in these industries between 1940-1990 are at highest risk, though exposure can occur in any building constructed before 1980.

Can I file a mesothelioma claim if the company went bankrupt?

Yes! Over 60 asbestos companies have established bankruptcy trust funds holding more than $30 billion specifically for victims. You can file claims against multiple trusts regardless of whether the company still exists. Georgia residents file approximately 780 trust fund claims annually. Trust claims are separate from lawsuits and can be filed simultaneously.

Am I eligible for VA benefits for mesothelioma?

If you served in the military and were exposed to asbestos during service, you may be eligible for VA disability benefits, healthcare, and special monthly compensation. Veterans with mesothelioma typically qualify for 100% disability rating ($3,800+/month). VA benefits do NOT reduce your eligibility for trust fund claims or lawsuit settlements. The VA has expedited processing for mesothelioma claims.

How much does a Georgia mesothelioma attorney cost?

Most mesothelioma attorneys in Georgia work on contingency, meaning you pay nothing upfront and no fees unless you win. Typical contingency fees range from 25-33% of the settlement. Given that the average settlement is $1.2M, the attorney fees are offset by significantly higher compensation amounts than pursuing claims without legal representation.
