Why Truck Accidents Result in Higher Settlements

  • Greater Impact Forces: A loaded semi-truck weighs up to 80,000 lbs — 20-30x the weight of a passenger car, causing far more severe injuries
  • Multiple Liable Parties: Truck driver, trucking company, cargo loader, vehicle manufacturer, and maintenance provider may all share liability
  • Higher Insurance Limits: Federal law requires commercial trucks to carry $750,000-$5,000,000 in liability insurance (vs. $25,000-$50,000 for personal vehicles)
  • Federal Regulations: Violations of FMCSA hours-of-service rules, ELD requirements, or DOT inspections strengthen negligence claims
  • Corporate Resources: Trucking companies and their insurers often have deep pockets, supporting larger settlements

Common Causes of Truck Accidents

  • Driver Fatigue: Truckers driving beyond hours-of-service limits or falsifying log books
  • Distracted Driving: Cell phone use, GPS adjustments, eating while driving
  • Improper Loading: Overweight or improperly secured cargo causing rollovers or spills
  • Mechanical Failure: Brake failure, tire blowouts, defective parts due to poor maintenance
  • Speeding / Reckless Driving: Pressure from dispatchers to meet tight delivery deadlines
  • DUI/DWI: Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs (including amphetamines)
  • Inadequate Training: Inexperienced drivers operating complex vehicles without proper certification

Key Evidence in Truck Accident Claims

  • Electronic Logging Device (ELD) Data: Shows hours driven, rest periods, and potential violations
  • Black Box / Event Data Recorder: Records speed, braking, and steering in the moments before a crash
  • Driver Qualification File: CDL status, driving history, drug/alcohol test results
  • Vehicle Inspection Reports: Pre-trip and DOT inspection records showing maintenance issues
  • Dispatch & Communication Records: Evidence of pressure to drive beyond legal limits
  • Dashcam & Surveillance Footage: Visual evidence of the accident and driver behavior

Steps After a Truck Accident

  • Seek Immediate Medical Attention: Document all injuries, even those that seem minor
  • Preserve Evidence: Photograph the scene, get witness contact information, save all medical records
  • Request a Police Report: Official documentation of the accident and any citations issued
  • Do Not Give Recorded Statements: Insurance companies may use your words against you
  • Contact a Truck Accident Attorney: Specialized attorneys understand FMCSA regulations and how to maximize claims
  • Act Quickly: Statute of limitations varies by state (typically 2-3 years), and critical evidence can be lost
