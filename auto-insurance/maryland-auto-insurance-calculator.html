❓ Maryland Auto Insurance FAQ

What is the minimum auto insurance required in Maryland?
Maryland requires minimum liability coverage of 30/60/15 (bodily injury per person/per accident/property damage in thousands). Personal Injury Protection (PIP) is also required. However, these minimums are often insufficient for serious accidents — most experts recommend at least 100/300/100.
How much does car insurance cost in Maryland?
The average car insurance premium in Maryland is approximately $1,950 per year ($163/month). Your actual rate depends on your age, driving record, vehicle, credit score, coverage level, and location within the state.
Is Maryland a no-fault or at-fault state?
Maryland is an at-fault (tort) state. The driver who causes the accident is financially responsible for the other party's injuries and property damage.
What is the uninsured motorist rate in Maryland?
Approximately 12.4% of drivers in Maryland are uninsured. This is near the national average of 12.6%. Uninsured motorist coverage is recommended.
Does Maryland require full coverage?
No, Maryland only requires liability insurance. However, if you have a car loan or lease, your lender will almost certainly require collision and comprehensive coverage ("full coverage"). Even if you own your car outright, full coverage is recommended for vehicles worth more than $5,000.
How can I get the cheapest car insurance in Maryland?
To get the cheapest rates in Maryland: compare quotes from at least 5 insurers, maintain a clean driving record, raise your deductible, bundle home and auto policies, ask about all available discounts (good student, low mileage, safe driver), and improve your credit score.
