❓ Minnesota Auto Insurance FAQ

What is the minimum auto insurance required in Minnesota?
Minnesota requires minimum liability coverage of 30/60/10 (bodily injury per person/per accident/property damage in thousands). Personal Injury Protection (PIP) is also required. However, these minimums are often insufficient for serious accidents — most experts recommend at least 100/300/100.
How much does car insurance cost in Minnesota?
The average car insurance premium in Minnesota is approximately $1,580 per year ($132/month). Your actual rate depends on your age, driving record, vehicle, credit score, coverage level, and location within the state.
Is Minnesota a no-fault or at-fault state?
Minnesota is a no-fault insurance state. After an accident, your own insurance company pays for your medical expenses regardless of who caused the crash.
What is the uninsured motorist rate in Minnesota?
Approximately 7.8% of drivers in Minnesota are uninsured. This is below the national average of 12.6%, but uninsured motorist coverage is still recommended for protection.
Does Minnesota require full coverage?
No, Minnesota only requires liability insurance. However, if you have a car loan or lease, your lender will almost certainly require collision and comprehensive coverage ("full coverage"). Even if you own your car outright, full coverage is recommended for vehicles worth more than $5,000.
How can I get the cheapest car insurance in Minnesota?
To get the cheapest rates in Minnesota: compare quotes from at least 5 insurers, maintain a clean driving record, raise your deductible, bundle home and auto policies, ask about all available discounts (good student, low mileage, safe driver), and improve your credit score.
