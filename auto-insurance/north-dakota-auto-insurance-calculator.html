❓ North Dakota Auto Insurance FAQ

What is the minimum auto insurance required in North Dakota?
North Dakota requires minimum liability coverage of 25/50/25 (bodily injury per person/per accident/property damage in thousands). Personal Injury Protection (PIP) is also required. However, these minimums are often insufficient for serious accidents — most experts recommend at least 100/300/100.
How much does car insurance cost in North Dakota?
The average car insurance premium in North Dakota is approximately $1,350 per year ($113/month). Your actual rate depends on your age, driving record, vehicle, credit score, coverage level, and location within the state.
Is North Dakota a no-fault or at-fault state?
North Dakota is a no-fault insurance state. After an accident, your own insurance company pays for your medical expenses regardless of who caused the crash.
What is the uninsured motorist rate in North Dakota?
Approximately 6.1% of drivers in North Dakota are uninsured. This is below the national average of 12.6%, but uninsured motorist coverage is still recommended for protection.
Does North Dakota require full coverage?
No, North Dakota only requires liability insurance. However, if you have a car loan or lease, your lender will almost certainly require collision and comprehensive coverage ("full coverage"). Even if you own your car outright, full coverage is recommended for vehicles worth more than $5,000.
How can I get the cheapest car insurance in North Dakota?
To get the cheapest rates in North Dakota: compare quotes from at least 5 insurers, maintain a clean driving record, raise your deductible, bundle home and auto policies, ask about all available discounts (good student, low mileage, safe driver), and improve your credit score.
