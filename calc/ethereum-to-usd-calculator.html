What is Ethereum (ETH)?

Ethereum is the world's second-largest cryptocurrency by market cap, behind Bitcoin. Unlike Bitcoin, which is primarily a digital currency, Ethereum is a decentralized platform that runs smart contracts — self-executing programs that run exactly as coded without downtime, censorship, fraud, or third-party interference.

Key differences from Bitcoin:

  • Purpose — Bitcoin is digital money; Ethereum is a programmable blockchain platform
  • Smart Contracts — Ethereum enables developers to build decentralized applications (dApps)
  • Supply — Bitcoin has a fixed 21M cap; ETH has no hard cap (but is deflationary post-EIP-1559)
  • Transaction Speed — Ethereum confirms in ~12 seconds vs Bitcoin's ~10 minutes
  • Use Cases — ETH powers DeFi (lending, trading), NFTs, DAOs, and more

Ethereum's Transition to Proof of Stake

In September 2022, Ethereum completed "The Merge" — transitioning from energy-intensive Proof of Work (mining) to eco-friendly Proof of Stake (staking). This monumental upgrade:

  • Reduced energy usage by 99.95% — Ethereum now uses less energy than Netflix
  • Made ETH deflationary — Transaction fees are burned, reducing supply (EIP-1559)
  • Enabled staking — Holders can stake 32 ETH to become validators and earn ~4-5% APY
  • Set stage for scaling — Sharding (planned) will increase transaction capacity by 100x

Since The Merge, ETH supply has been deflationary (burning more ETH than issued), which many analysts believe is bullish for price long-term.

Ethereum Use Cases

ETH isn't just a cryptocurrency — it's the fuel that powers a massive ecosystem of applications:

  • Decentralized Finance (DeFi) — Lend, borrow, trade without banks (Aave, Compound, Uniswap)
  • NFTs — Digital art, collectibles, gaming items (OpenSea, Blur)
  • Stablecoins — USDC, DAI run on Ethereum; $100B+ in circulation
  • DAOs — Member-owned organizations governed by smart contracts
  • Gaming — Play-to-earn, virtual worlds (Axie Infinity, Decentraland)
  • Identity — Self-sovereign digital identity (ENS, Proof of Humanity)
  • Supply Chain — Track goods, verify authenticity (VeChain uses Ethereum tech)

Over 500,000 active developers build on Ethereum — more than any other blockchain.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the ETH to USD price determined?
ETH price is determined by supply and demand across global cryptocurrency exchanges. Prices vary slightly between exchanges due to liquidity and trading volume. Most calculators (including this one) use an average of major exchanges like Coinbase, Kraken, and Binance.
Is Ethereum a good investment?
Ethereum has historically been one of the best-performing assets (10,000%+ returns since 2015), but it's also extremely volatile. It dropped 94% in 2018 and 82% in 2022. Only invest what you can afford to lose. Many analysts are bullish on ETH long-term due to: staking yield, deflationary tokenomics post-Merge, and growing real-world usage in DeFi/NFTs.
How much does it cost to send Ethereum?
Ethereum transaction fees (gas) vary based on network congestion. During low activity: $0.50-$2. During high activity (bull markets, NFT drops): $10-$100+. Layer 2 solutions (Arbitrum, Optimism) offer the same security with fees under $0.10.
Can I mine Ethereum?
No. Ethereum mining ended in September 2022 with The Merge. The network now uses Proof of Stake. You can still earn ETH by staking (locking up 32 ETH to validate transactions) or using liquid staking services like Lido or Rocket Pool (stake any amount).
What's the difference between ETH and Bitcoin?
Bitcoin is primarily a store of value ("digital gold"). Ethereum is a programmable platform for applications. Bitcoin has a 21M supply cap and confirms transactions in ~10 minutes. Ethereum has no hard cap (but is deflationary) and confirms in ~12 seconds. ETH is more versatile; BTC is more established as a store of value.
How do I buy Ethereum?
1) Sign up for an exchange (Coinbase, Kraken, Gemini). 2) Verify your identity (KYC). 3) Deposit USD via bank transfer or debit card. 4) Buy ETH. 5) Optionally transfer to a self-custody wallet (MetaMask, Ledger) for security. Most beginners start with Coinbase due to its simple interface.
Is it too late to invest in Ethereum?
Ethereum is still early relative to mainstream adoption. While it's no longer $0.31 (2015 price), the ecosystem is growing rapidly. Institutional adoption is increasing (BlackRock, JPMorgan building on Ethereum). If you believe blockchain will transform finance/internet, ETH has potential. But remember: crypto is high-risk, and past performance doesn't guarantee future returns.
Will Ethereum surpass Bitcoin?
Possible but uncertain. Bitcoin has 2x the market cap (~$1.3T vs ETH's ~$400B) and is more established as "digital gold." However, Ethereum has more real-world utility (DeFi, NFTs, apps) and better tokenomics post-Merge (deflationary supply). Some analysts predict "The Flippening" (ETH overtaking BTC in market cap) could happen if Ethereum scales successfully.
