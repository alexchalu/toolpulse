About Natural Gas Costs

Natural gas is measured in therms (1 therm = 100,000 BTU). Understanding your gas usage helps you identify high-consumption appliances and opportunities to save money.

Average Natural Gas Consumption

  • Heating-dominated climate: 80-120 therms/month in winter
  • Moderate climate: 40-60 therms/month
  • Warm climate: 10-30 therms/month
  • Summer (hot water only): 10-20 therms/month

Tips to Reduce Natural Gas Bills

  • Lower thermostat by 2-3°F (can save 5-10% on heating)
  • Install programmable thermostat
  • Seal air leaks around windows and doors
  • Insulate attic and walls
  • Service furnace annually
  • Lower water heater temperature to 120°F
  • Insulate water heater tank and hot water pipes
  • Use cold water for laundry when possible

Understanding Your Gas Bill

Natural gas bills typically include:

  • Supply charge: The cost per therm of gas consumed
  • Delivery charge: Infrastructure and distribution costs
  • Customer charge: Fixed monthly fee for service
  • Taxes and fees: Vary by location
