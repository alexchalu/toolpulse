    📊 Understanding Home Energy Costs

    Heating Degree Days (HDD)

    Measures heating needs based on outdoor temps below 65°F. Cold climates (7,000+ HDD) like Minnesota have much higher heating costs than mild climates (2,000 HDD) like Georgia.

    Fuel Cost Comparison (2026)

    • Natural Gas: $1.00-1.50/therm ($10-15/MMBtu) — cheapest in most markets
    • Propane: $2.50-3.50/gallon ($25-35/MMBtu) — 2-3x more expensive than gas
    • Heating Oil: $3.00-4.50/gallon ($21-32/MMBtu) — volatile, regional pricing
    • Electric Resistance: $0.12-0.20/kWh ($35-60/MMBtu) — most expensive unless heat pump
    • Heat Pump: Effective $12-20/MMBtu equivalent (200-300% efficiency) — competitive with gas

    System Efficiency Ratings

    • AFUE (Furnaces): 80% (old) to 98% (condensing gas furnace)
    • HSPF (Heat Pumps): 8-13 (heating), higher is better
    • SEER (Cooling): 13-25 (cooling), minimum 14 for new units since 2023
    • COP (Heat Pumps): 2.0-4.0 (200-400% efficiency), varies with outdoor temp
