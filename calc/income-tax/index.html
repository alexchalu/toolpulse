How State Income Tax Works

  • Progressive vs Flat: Most states use progressive brackets (higher rates for higher income). Some states like Illinois use a flat rate.
  • Standard Deductions: Most states offer standard deductions similar to federal (varies by state).
  • Local Taxes: Some cities (NYC, Yonkers) impose additional local income taxes.
  • Federal vs State: Federal income tax is separate and applies nationwide. State tax is additional.

Moving for Tax Savings?

Consider the full picture before relocating:

  • Income tax savings: Can be $5K-$15K+ annually for high earners
  • Property taxes: TX/FL have higher property taxes than some income-tax states
  • Sales tax: No-income-tax states often have higher sales taxes
  • Cost of living: Housing, food, utilities vary widely by state
  • Quality of life: Schools, healthcare, climate, job market matter too

📐 How We Calculate This

Our calculator uses the latest federal and state tax brackets published by the IRS and state revenue departments. We apply standard deductions, personal exemptions, and marginal tax rates for the current tax year.

Results account for filing status, income level, and applicable credits. We update our tax data within 48 hours of any IRS or state announcements. For complex situations (AMT, self-employment, capital gains), consult a licensed CPA.
