Understanding Virginia State Income Tax in 2026

Virginia uses a four-bracket progressive income tax system with rates ranging from 2% to 5.75%. As the Commonwealth of Virginia, the state offers a relatively moderate tax burden compared to high-tax states like California and New York, while providing more revenue for state services than zero-income-tax states.

This comprehensive guide will help you understand Virginia's tax structure, calculate your estimated tax liability, and discover strategies to minimize your tax burden legally.

2026 Virginia State Tax Brackets

Unlike most states that vary brackets by filing status, Virginia uses the same income thresholds for single filers, married filing jointly, and married filing separately. Only the standard deduction differs by filing status.

Taxable Income Range Tax Rate Tax Owed
$0 - $3,000 2.00% 2% of taxable income
$3,001 - $5,000 3.00% $60 + 3% of excess over $3,000
$5,001 - $17,000 5.00% $120 + 5% of excess over $5,000
Over $17,000 5.75% $720 + 5.75% of excess over $17,000

Virginia Standard Deductions for 2026

The standard deduction reduces your taxable income before applying tax rates:

  • Single: $8,000
  • Married Filing Jointly: $16,000
  • Married Filing Separately: $8,000

Virginia does not have a separate head of household filing status at the state level. If you file as head of household federally, you'll file as single for Virginia purposes and use the $8,000 standard deduction.

How to Calculate Your Virginia Income Tax

Follow these steps to calculate your VA state income tax:

  1. Start with federal adjusted gross income (AGI) - From your federal Form 1040
  2. Make Virginia additions and subtractions - Adjust for items that differ between federal and state tax treatment
  3. Calculate Virginia AGI - Federal AGI +/- Virginia adjustments
  4. Subtract deductions - Standard deduction ($8,000 or $16,000) or itemized deductions
  5. Determine taxable income - Virginia AGI minus deductions
  6. Apply tax brackets - Use the four-bracket progressive system
  7. Subtract tax credits - Including earned income credit, low-income credit, etc.
  8. Calculate final tax liability - Total Virginia income tax owed

Virginia vs. Federal Tax Differences

While Virginia tax largely follows federal tax rules, several important differences exist:

  • Standard Deduction: VA amounts ($8,000/$16,000) differ from federal standard deductions
  • Social Security Benefits: Virginia does not tax Social Security retirement benefits
  • Military Pay: $15,000 exemption for military basic pay for Virginia residents
  • 529 Plans: Up to $4,000 per account deduction for Virginia 529 contributions (no age limit)
  • State Tax Deduction: Unlike federal SALT cap, you can deduct state/local taxes if itemizing
  • Age Deduction: $12,000 deduction for taxpayers 65 and older (income limits apply)

Virginia Tax Credits

Virginia offers several tax credits that can reduce your tax liability:

  • Low Income Credit: Up to $300 for individuals, $600 for married couples (income limits apply)
  • Earned Income Tax Credit: 20% of the federal EITC for qualifying families
  • Age Deduction: $12,000 for those 65+ with income under $50,000 (single) or $75,000 (married)
  • Land Preservation Credit: For donating conservation easements
  • Historic Rehabilitation Credit: For restoring historic properties
  • Education Improvement Credit: For donations to scholarship foundations

Northern Virginia Considerations

If you live or work in Northern Virginia (Arlington, Fairfax, Loudoun counties, etc.), be aware of these factors:

  • No local income tax: Unlike Maryland (county tax) or D.C., Virginia has no local income taxes
  • High property tax trade-off: Lower income tax but higher property taxes in NoVA
  • D.C. commuters: If you work in D.C., you'll pay D.C. income tax (8.5%-10.75%) and get a credit on your VA return
  • Federal employees: Large federal workforce qualifies for specific deductions and credits
  • Cost of living: High incomes in NoVA mean most taxpayers hit the 5.75% top bracket

Tax Strategies for Virginia Residents

Consider these strategies to reduce your Virginia state tax liability:

  • Maximize Virginia 529 Contributions: Deduct up to $4,000 per account with no age limit or income restriction
  • Consider Itemizing: If federal itemized deductions exceed $8,000 (single) or $16,000 (married), itemize in Virginia too
  • Retirement Contributions: Traditional IRA and 401(k) contributions reduce Virginia taxable income
  • Age 65+ Deduction: If eligible, claim the $12,000 age deduction to significantly reduce taxable income
  • HSA Contributions: Health Savings Account contributions reduce both federal and Virginia income
  • Military Benefits: Active duty and veterans should explore all available exemptions and credits
  • Timing Income: If near the $17,000 threshold, timing income/deductions can affect your marginal rate

Retiree Advantages in Virginia

Virginia offers several benefits for retirees:

  • No Social Security Tax: 100% of Social Security benefits are exempt from Virginia income tax
  • Age Deduction: $12,000 deduction for those 65+ with income under limits
  • Military Retirement: Up to $10,000 subtraction for military retirement pay (or $40,000 if age 55+ or disabled)
  • Moderate Tax Rates: Even without special exemptions, 5.75% top rate is reasonable for retirees
  • No Estate Tax: Virginia does not impose state estate or inheritance tax

When to File and Pay

Important dates for Virginia state income tax:

  • Tax Return Due Date: May 1, 2027 (for 2026 tax year) - later than federal April 15
  • Extension Deadline: November 1, 2027 (automatic 6-month extension from May 1)
  • Estimated Tax Payments: Due quarterly (May 1, June 15, September 15, January 15)
  • Payment Due: Full payment due May 1, even if you file an extension

Virginia's May 1 deadline gives you an extra two weeks beyond the federal deadline to prepare your state return.

High-Earner Considerations

If you're a high-income earner in Virginia:

  • The 5.75% top bracket kicks in at just $17,000 of taxable income - nearly everyone pays this rate
  • Combined federal + state rates can reach 42.75% (37% federal + 5.75% state) for top earners
  • No additional tax on capital gains - taxed as ordinary income at your bracket rate
  • Consider tax-loss harvesting to offset capital gains
  • Northern Virginia executives should explore all deductions given high cost of living
  • If considering relocation, compare Virginia (5.75%) to Florida (0%), North Carolina (4.75%), or Tennessee (0%)

What is the Virginia state income tax rate for 2026?

Virginia has progressive tax rates ranging from 2% to 5.75%. The rates are: 2% on income up to $3,000, 3% on $3,001-$5,000, 5% on $5,001-$17,000, and 5.75% on income over $17,000. These brackets are the same for all filing statuses.

How do I calculate my Virginia state income tax?

To calculate VA state income tax: 1) Determine your Virginia adjusted gross income, 2) Subtract your standard deduction ($8,000 single, $16,000 married), 3) Apply the progressive tax brackets, 4) Subtract any tax credits you qualify for. Use our calculator above for instant results.

What is the Virginia standard deduction for 2026?

For 2026, the Virginia standard deduction is $8,000 for single filers and $16,000 for married filing jointly. Virginia does not offer different amounts for head of household status.

Does Virginia have local income taxes?

No, Virginia does not have county or city income taxes. Only state income tax applies, making tax calculations simpler than states like Ohio or Pennsylvania with local taxes.

When is Virginia state income tax due?

Virginia state income tax returns are due on May 1st (later than the federal April 15 deadline). Extensions are available until November 1st, but payment is still due May 1st to avoid penalties and interest.

Does Virginia tax Social Security benefits?

No, Virginia does not tax Social Security retirement benefits, making it more favorable for retirees. Combined with the age deduction ($12,000 for those 65+), Virginia can be tax-friendly for retirees.

Can I deduct Virginia 529 contributions?

Yes, Virginia allows a deduction of up to $4,000 per account per year for contributions to Virginia 529 plans. There is no income limit or age restriction for this deduction, and unused amounts can carry forward indefinitely.

How does Virginia tax military retirement pay?

Virginia offers a subtraction for military retirement pay. The amount is up to $10,000, or up to $40,000 if you're age 55 or older or receiving disability retirement. Active duty military can also subtract up to $15,000 of basic pay.
