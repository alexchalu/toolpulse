What is DSCR?

Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) measures how many times your net operating income can cover your debt payments. It's one of the most important metrics lenders use to assess commercial real estate loans and business loans.

Formula: DSCR = Net Operating Income (NOI) ÷ Annual Debt Service

Understanding DSCR Results

  • DSCR > 1.25: Excellent - Most commercial lenders' minimum requirement. Shows strong cash flow cushion.
  • DSCR = 1.20-1.24: Good - Acceptable to many lenders, but may result in higher rates or lower LTV.
  • DSCR = 1.10-1.19: Marginal - May qualify for some hard money or specialized lenders.
  • DSCR = 1.00-1.09: Risky - Property barely covers debt. Very difficult to get conventional financing.
  • DSCR < 1.00: Negative cash flow - Property doesn't generate enough income to cover debt payments. Will not qualify for most loans.

How to Improve Your DSCR

Increase Net Operating Income:

  • Raise rents: Increase to market rates if below-market
  • Reduce vacancy: Improve marketing, property appeal, tenant retention
  • Add revenue streams: Parking fees, storage rentals, laundry, pet fees
  • Reduce operating expenses: Negotiate better insurance rates, utility costs, management fees
  • Improve efficiency: Energy-efficient upgrades reduce utility costs

Reduce Debt Service:

  • Make larger down payment: Lower loan amount = lower monthly payment
  • Extend loan term: 25 years vs. 20 years reduces payment (but increases total interest)
  • Negotiate better rate: Shop multiple lenders, improve credit score
  • Pay down existing debt: Eliminate or reduce other loans on the property
  • Interest-only period: Some loans offer initial interest-only payments (increases DSCR temporarily)

Common DSCR Mistakes

  • Including principal paydown in NOI: NOI should exclude all debt service (principal + interest)
  • Forgetting property taxes and insurance: If not escrowed, these reduce NOI
  • Using gross income instead of NOI: Must subtract all operating expenses first
  • Not accounting for vacancy: Use effective gross income (actual collected rent) not potential
  • Ignoring capital reserves: Lenders often require 5-10% reserves deducted from NOI
  • Using pro forma instead of actual: Lenders base DSCR on current financials, not projections

DSCR Loan (No Income Verification)

A DSCR loan is a type of investment property mortgage that qualifies borrowers based on the property's cash flow rather than personal income. Key features:

  • No tax returns, W-2s, or pay stubs required
  • Perfect for self-employed investors, retirees, or those with complex income
  • Minimum DSCR typically 1.00x (some lenders go to 0.75x with higher rates)
  • Rates typically 0.5-1.5% higher than traditional investor loans
  • Available for 1-4 unit properties and small multifamily (5-8 units)
  • Requires 15-25% down payment

Example DSCR Calculation

Scenario: 8-Unit Apartment Building

Gross Rental Income: $96,000/year (8 units × $1,000/month)

Operating Expenses:

  • Property taxes: $8,000
  • Insurance: $3,000
  • Maintenance & repairs: $5,000
  • Property management (8%): $7,680
  • Utilities (owner-paid): $4,000
  • Reserves: $3,000
  • Total Operating Expenses: $30,680

Net Operating Income (NOI): $96,000 - $30,680 = $65,320

Annual Debt Service: $52,000 ($4,333/month payment)

DSCR: $65,320 ÷ $52,000 = 1.26x

✓ This property would qualify for most commercial loans (DSCR > 1.25x)
