How Commercial Property Loans Work

Commercial property loans (also called commercial mortgages) are used to purchase or refinance income-producing real estate such as office buildings, retail centers, warehouses, apartment complexes, and industrial properties. These loans typically have:

  • Higher down payments: Usually 20-35% compared to 3-20% for residential mortgages
  • Shorter terms: Most commonly 5, 7, 10, or 20 years vs. 30 years for residential
  • Higher interest rates: Typically 0.5-2% higher than residential mortgage rates
  • Balloon payments: Many have amortization periods longer than the loan term, requiring refinancing
  • Stricter qualifications: Lenders evaluate property cash flow, debt service coverage ratio (DSCR), and borrower experience

Understanding Your Results

Monthly Payment: Your principal and interest payment. Note that commercial loans may also require property taxes, insurance, and CAM (Common Area Maintenance) charges paid separately.

Total Interest: The total amount you'll pay in interest over the life of the loan. This can equal or exceed your loan amount for longer terms.

Loan-to-Value (LTV): The ratio of your loan amount to the property's purchase price. Most commercial lenders require LTV of 65-80%.

Amortization Schedule: Shows how your payment is split between principal and interest each month, and your remaining balance over time.

Types of Commercial Property Loans

  • Traditional Bank Loans: Fixed or variable rates, 5-25 year terms, best rates for qualified borrowers
  • SBA 504 Loans: Government-backed, up to 90% LTV, fixed rates, for owner-occupied properties
  • SBA 7(a) Loans: Up to $5M, flexible use, 10-25 year terms
  • CMBS Loans: Commercial Mortgage-Backed Securities, fixed rates, non-recourse, for properties $2M+
  • Bridge Loans: Short-term (6-36 months), higher rates, for quick closings or repositioning
  • Hard Money Loans: Asset-based, fast approval, 8-15% rates, for investors with challenged credit

Key Factors Affecting Commercial Loan Rates

  • Property Type: Office and retail properties typically get better rates than hotels or specialty properties
  • Property Condition & Occupancy: Well-maintained, fully leased properties qualify for lower rates
  • Location: Properties in strong markets with diverse economies get preferential pricing
  • Borrower Experience: Experienced commercial property owners get better terms
  • Cash Flow (DSCR): Lenders want debt service coverage ratio of 1.25x or higher
  • Down Payment: Larger down payments (lower LTV) result in better rates
  • Loan Amount: Larger loans ($1M+) often qualify for better pricing

Commercial vs. Residential Mortgages

Feature Commercial Residential
Down Payment 20-35% 3-20%
Typical Term 5-20 years 15-30 years
Interest Rate 5-9% 3-7%
Qualification Property cash flow + borrower Borrower income & credit
Recourse Often recourse (personal guarantee) Non-recourse (except fraud)
Prepayment Penalty Common (lockout, yield maintenance) Rare

Tips for Getting the Best Commercial Loan

  • Improve property performance: Higher occupancy and net operating income (NOI) means better loan terms
  • Shop multiple lenders: Rates and terms can vary significantly between banks, credit unions, and private lenders
  • Strengthen your financials: Strong personal and business credit, significant reserves, and low debt-to-income ratios help
  • Consider an SBA loan: If owner-occupied (51%+ use), SBA 504 or 7(a) loans offer better terms than conventional
  • Negotiate terms: Interest rate, amortization period, prepayment penalties, and recourse provisions are all negotiable
  • Plan for refinancing: Many commercial loans have balloon payments requiring refinance before maturity
  • Work with a broker: Commercial mortgage brokers have access to multiple lenders and can negotiate better terms
