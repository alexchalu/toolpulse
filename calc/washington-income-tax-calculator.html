Why Washington Has No State Income Tax

Washington is one of only 9 states with NO state income tax. The Washington Constitution prohibits a state income tax without voter approval via constitutional amendment.

How does Washington fund state government without income tax?

  • Sales tax: 6.25% state rate (local jurisdictions add 0-2%, total up to 8.25%)
  • Property tax: Among the highest in the nation (~1.6-1.8% of home value annually)
  • Business franchise tax: Tax on business margins (not on individuals)
  • Oil & gas severance taxes: Revenue from Washington's massive energy industry
  • Federal transfers: Grants and highway funds

The lack of state income tax makes Washington attractive to high earners, retirees, and businesses.

Who Benefits Most from No Income Tax in Washington?

  • High earners ($200k+) — Save $10,000-50,000+/year vs high-tax states
  • Retirees — Social Security + pension income not taxed at state level
  • Remote workers from high-tax states — Keep your CA/NY salary, pay $0 state tax
  • Business owners — Pass-through income (S-corp, LLC) not subject to state income tax
  • Investors — Capital gains, dividends, interest 100% state-tax-free

Who pays more in Washington? Homeowners with expensive properties (due to high property taxes) and low-income renters (sales tax is regressive).

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Washington have state income tax?
No. Washington is one of 9 states with NO state income tax. The Washington Constitution prohibits it without voter approval via constitutional amendment.
How much do I save by living in Washington instead of California?
Depends on income. A $100k earner saves ~$5,900/year. A $250k earner saves ~$20,900/year. Over 30 years, that's $177,000-627,000 in savings (not accounting for investment growth).
Do I have to file a Washington state tax return?
No. Since Washington has no income tax, there is NO state tax return to file. You only file federal taxes (IRS Form 1040).
What is the sales tax rate in Washington?
6.25% state sales tax. Local jurisdictions (cities, counties, transit authorities) can add up to 2%, bringing the total to 6.25%-8.25% depending on location.
Are Washington property taxes really that high?
Yes. Washington has the 6th-highest property tax rate in the U.S. at ~1.6-1.8% of assessed value. On a $300,000 home, expect $4,800-5,400/year. This partially offsets the savings from no income tax.
Does Washington tax retirement income?
No. Social Security, pensions, IRA/401(k) withdrawals, and annuities are NOT taxed at the state level. This makes Washington very attractive for retirees.
Can I move to Washington to avoid state income tax?
Yes, but you must establish true residency (live there >183 days/year, get a WA driver's license, register to vote, etc.). Some high-tax states (like California) aggressively audit people who claim to have moved to avoid taxes.
Will Washington ever get a state income tax?
Unlikely. It would require a constitutional amendment approved by voters. Washington residents have historically opposed income tax by wide margins (70%+ against in polls). Political culture strongly favors keeping the state income-tax-free.
