## 6. Newark Zoning Information

### Zoning District
The LoopNet property record shows nearby properties zoned **M-1**, **M-2**, and **C2**, suggesting the McCarter Highway corridor in this area is zoned for industrial/manufacturing use.

### Newark Zoning Ordinance (Title XLI)
- **Industrial Districts in Newark:** I-1 (Light Industrial), I-2 (Heavy Industrial), I-3 (Port/Airport)
- Per Newark's 2023 zoning ordinance (Ord. No. 6PSF-E), § 41:4-3 governs **Permitted Uses in Industrial, Airport, and Port Area Districts**
- Industrial outdoor storage is generally a permitted use in I-2 and I-3 zones
- The McCarter Highway / Route 21 corridor between the Passaic River and residential neighborhoods typically falls in **I-1 or I-2** zoning
- Newark Zoning Maps: https://ecode360.com/36740476

### Key Zoning Considerations
- **Flood Zone AE** — may trigger FEMA requirements for improvements
- Property bordered by Route 21 overpass — infrastructure constraints
- Nonconforming use protections apply under Newark zoning ordinance
- Industrial outdoor storage (fleet parking, container storage) is an established use in this area

## 10. Key Risk Factors

1. **Flood Zone AE** — FEMA floodplain designation increases insurance costs and limits development
2. **Environmental** — Industrial corridor; Phase I/II ESA warranted
3. **Market Softening** — Newark industrial asking rents declining >12% YoY; vacancy rising
4. **Tariff Uncertainty** — Port-centric demand impacted by trade policy (imports declining)
5. **Limited Improvements** — Mostly open land; revenue depends on IOS/parking demand
6. **Discrepancy in Lot Size** — Tax records show 4.83 AC vs. marketing at 6.5–6.9 AC (may include adjacent lots or ROW)
7. **Route 21 Infrastructure** — Overpass borders property; access constraints possible
