## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

251 Monroe Avenue is an approximately **45-acre** multi-tenant industrial site located in the Borough of Kenilworth, Union County, New Jersey. The property features **267,188 SF of industrial/R&D buildings** originally constructed in 1954, positioned along the Route 22 East corridor with excellent access to I-78, the NJ Turnpike, and Port Newark/Elizabeth.

The site is currently **multi-tenanted** with a diverse roster including **Doka Group** (formwork/construction, ~19 acres — anchor tenant), **Durham School Services** (school bus operations, ~2.42 acres), **FDR Hitches** (18,000 SF), **PSEG** (utility pole storage, ~1 acre), and **Pinnacle Cosmetics** (license agreement). The property is zoned **"I" Industrial** in the Borough of Kenilworth with permitted uses including warehouse, manufacturing, and outdoor storage.

**Key Investment Highlights:**
- 45-acre industrial campus with diversified tenant base
- Anchor tenant (Doka) on 15+ year term at $3.47M+ annual rent with 3% escalations
- Total estimated gross income exceeding $5.0M annually
- Route 22/I-78 corridor — prime Northern NJ industrial location
- Multiple value-add opportunities: 88 additional parking spaces available, potential redevelopment on underutilized portions

## STRENGTHS

1. **Diversified tenant base** — 5 tenants across different industries (construction, transportation, utilities, cosmetics) reduces concentration risk
2. **Strong anchor tenant** — Doka (Umdasch Group) is a global leader with national HQ commitment, 15-year term
3. **Built-in rent growth** — Doka 20% step-up in 2027 + 3% annual; Durham 4% annual; FDR 3% annual
4. **Scale** — 45-acre site is rare and difficult to replicate in Northern NJ
5. **Location** — Route 22/I-78 corridor with excellent logistics access
6. **Zoning** — Industrial as of right with site plan approvals in place
7. **Credit quality** — Doka (Umdasch Group), Durham (National Express), PSEG (NYSE: PEG) are all investment-grade or near-investment-grade entities

---

## RISKS & CONCERNS

1. **Building age** — 1954 construction may require significant capital expenditure. PCA report should be reviewed in detail.
2. **PSEG short-term** — 13-month lease with only 6-month extension option. Need plan for 1-acre vacancy in 2027.
3. **Pinnacle Cosmetics** — License (not lease) structure provides less security; multiple amendments suggest evolving terms.
4. **Parking deficit** — 88 spaces short of code. While correctable by striping, represents a zoning compliance risk.
5. **Municipal boundary** — Property spans Kenilworth and Union Township, creating potential jurisdictional complexity.
6. **Castle and Cooke environmental legacy** — Former owner was major agricultural/industrial company. Potential for legacy contamination.
7. **Doka termination right** — Section 37.08 allows early termination per Tenant's Notice of Termination. Terms and penalties to be verified.
8. **ISRA obligations** — NJ Industrial Site Recovery Act applies. Environmental clearance required on transfer.

## RECOMMENDATION

**Favorable — proceed to Phase II diligence.** The 251 Monroe campus presents a compelling multi-tenant industrial asset with strong fundamentals:

- **$5.27M current NOI** with clear path to **$6.5-7.0M by 2028-2030** through contractual escalations
- **Doka's 15-year anchor lease** provides stability and cash flow certainty
- **45 acres in the Route 22 corridor** is an irreplaceable asset — land scarcity in Northern NJ supports long-term value

**Key Phase II priorities:**
1. **Full PCA review** — 1954 building condition, deferred maintenance, capital needs
2. **Phase I/II Environmental** — ISRA compliance status, pre-existing contamination
3. **Doka termination clause** — Exact terms of Section 37.08 (penalties, notice period, conditions)
4. **Pinnacle Cosmetics license** — Full terms from 4 amendments
5. **Tax assessment verification** — Multi-municipal property requires careful tax analysis
6. **Parking compliance plan** — Cost and timeline to stripe 88 additional spaces

**Pricing guidance:** At current NOI of ~$5.27M, the property values at **$88-96M** at a 5.5-6.0% cap rate. However, with the Doka step-up in 2027 driving NOI to ~$6.0M+, forward cap rate pricing could support **$100-110M** on a stabilized basis.
