FOURTH AMENDMENT OF LEASE
This FOURTH AMENDMENT OF LEASE (this “Fourth Amendment”) is effective as of
the date upon which it has been signed by both parties (the “Effective Date”), by and between 76
CENTRAL OWNER LLC, having an office at c/o Criterion Group at 28-18 Steinway Street,
Astoria, NY 11103 (“Landlord”), and PORT KEARNY SECURITY, INC., having a mailing
address at 41 Doremus Avenue, Newark, New Jersey 07105 (“Tenant”).
RECITALS
WHEREAS, Landlord and Tenant are parties to that certain Lease, dated as of August 20,
2021, as amended by that certain First Amendment to Lease, dated as of November 5, 2021, that
certain Second Amendment to Lease, dated March 9, 2022 and that certain Third Amendment to
Lease, dated October 2023 (collectively, the “Original Lease”, and as further amended by this
Fourth Amendment, the “Lease”), pursuant to which Landlord leases to Tenant and Tenant leases
from Landlord certain premises consisting of approximately eight (8) acres of land (the “Demised
Premises”) located at and commonly known as 76 Central Avenue, Kearny, New Jersey (the
“Property”);
WHEREAS, Tenant has failed to pay Rent due under the Lease for multiple calendar
months, and is in arrears under the Lease in the amount of Four Hundred Fourteen Thousand Four
Hundred Fifty Six and 80/100 Dollars ($414,456.80) (the “Arrears Amount”);
WHEREAS, as a condition precedent to the effectiveness of this Fourth Amendment,
within three (3) days of the Effective Date, Tenant shall pay to Landlord (i) the amount of Two
Hundred Seven Thousand Two Hundred Twenty Eight and 40/100 Dollars ($207,228.40) (such
amount, the “Partial Catchup Payment”), and (ii) Fixed Rent and Additional Charges due under
the Lease as amended herein for the thirty-fourth (34th) month of the Term (January 2025)
(“January Rent”), which total amount of January Rent due is One Hundred Sixty-Seven Thousand
Three Hundred Eighty One and 86/100 Dollars ($167,381.86);
WHEREAS, Tenant shall pay to Landlord the remainder of the Arrears Amount (i.e., the
balance of the Arrears Amount after subtracting the Partial Catchup Payment therefrom), which is
equal to Two Hundred Seven Thousand Two Hundred Twenty Eight and 40/100 Dollars
($207,228.40), in ninety two (92) equal monthly installments of Two Thousand Two Hundred
Fifty Two and 48/100 Dollars ($2,252.48) (collectively, the “Installment Payments”) for the
remainder of the Term; and
WHEREAS, subject to the terms and conditions of this Fourth Amendment, in exchange
for Tenant’s payment of the Partial Catchup Payment, January Rent and the Installment Payments,
Landlord has agreed to grant Tenant an abatement of Fixed Rent due under the Lease for the
remainder of the Term, beginning on the first day of the thirty-fourth (34th) month of the Term
(January 1, 2025) through the Expiration Date (the “Abatement”).

Installment Payments and Partial Catchup Payment.
(a)

Tenant shall pay without demand, in advance on the first day of each and every
calendar month during the Term, the Installment Payments, which shall be
considered Additional Charges under the Lease. Tenant’s failure to pay to Landlord
any Installment Payment as aforesaid shall be an immediate default under the Lease
without the need for notice, entitling Landlord to all rights and remedies set forth
therein.

(b)

Notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained in the Original Lease, Landlord
and Tenant hereby agree that a condition precedent to the effectiveness of this
Fourth Amendment shall be that, within three (3) days of the Effective Date, Tenant
pays to Landlord (i) the Partial Catchup Payment and (ii) January Rent. If Tenant
fails to pay to Landlord the Partial Catchup Payment or January Rent within three
(3) days of the Effective Date, that shall be an immediate default under the Lease
without the need for notice, entitling Landlord to all rights and remedies set forth
therein.

4.

Default. Except to the extent set forth in Section 2 above, this Amendment shall not affect
any provision of the Original Lease governing payment of Rent, including, by way of
example and not limitation, any provision relating to the payment of Additional Charges,
the timing and manner of payment, late fees or other charges, or the rights of Landlord in
the event of non-payment or non-performance by Tenant. If there is default by Tenant
under the Lease beyond the applicable notice and cure period, the Abatement shall
immediately be null and void and the Fixed Rent due from Tenant shall revert to the amount
due under the Original Lease (without the Abatement) upon notice from Landlord. Further,
upon such default, the following sum shall be immediately due and payable to Landlord:
(i) the amount of the difference between (x) Fixed Rent due under the Original Lease for
the period between the first day of the Abatement and the date of the default and (y) Fixed
Rent due under the Lease for the period between the first day of the Abatement and the
date of the default, as reduced by the Abatement; and (ii) the sum of the remaining
Installment Payments. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary, if there is a default by
Tenant under the Lease beyond the applicable notice and cure period, as part of the
damages that Landlord is entitled to recover that are described in Section 27 of the Original
Lease and elsewhere, Landlord shall be entitled to recover from Tenant the full amount of
unpaid Rent due under the Original Lease, without regard to the Abatement granted in this
Fourth Amendment, and regardless of when such default occurs.

5.

Marketing of Demised Premises. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained in
the Original Lease, including, without limitation, Section 19.01 thereof, provided Landlord
provides Tenant with at least twenty-four (24) hours prior written notice (which may be
via electronic mail to msarcona@sarcona.com), Landlord and its agents shall have the right

6.

Landlord Termination Option. If Landlord shall obtain a suitable replacement tenant for
the Demised Premises, in Landlord’s sole judgment and without any duty on the part of
Landlord to market the Demised Premises, Landlord shall have the option, upon no less
than ninety (90) days’ prior written notice to Tenant (the “Termination Notice”) to
terminate the Lease. If given, the Termination Notice shall set forth the date upon which
the Lease shall terminate, which date must be at least ninety (90) days following the date
the Termination Notice is delivered to Tenant (the “Termination Date”) and the Lease shall
terminate on the Termination Date and the parties shall be released from all further
obligations to each other under the Lease except for those that expressly survive the
termination or expiration of the Lease.

7.

Tenant Additional Obligations. Within fifteen (15) days of the Effective Date, Tenant shall
deliver to Landlord (i) a fully executed estoppel certificate, which shall be in a form
acceptable to Landlord and Landlord’s lender or Mortgagee, subject to commercially
reasonable revisions requested by Tenant, and (ii) a fully executed SNDA, which shall be
in a form required by Landlord and Landlord’s lender or Mortgagee, subject to
commercially reasonable revisions requested by Tenant, and (iii) audited and signed
financial statements of Tenant for the prior two (2) calendar years, prepared by a certified
public accountant, tax returns of Tenant for the prior two (2) calendar years, and any other
financial information reasonably requested by Landlord or its lender or Mortgagee
(collectively, the “Financial Information”). Tenant’s failure to deliver to Landlord the
estoppel, the SNDA or the Financial Information required hereunder within fifteen (15)
days of the Effective Date shall be an immediate default under the Lease without the need
for notice, entitling Landlord to all rights and remedies set forth therein.

8.

Lease Ratified. Except to the extent hereinabove modified, the Original Lease is hereby
ratified and confirmed in all respects.

9.

Conflicts. In the event of a conflict or inconsistency between this Fourth Amendment and
the Original Lease, the terms of this Fourth Amendment shall supersede and govern.

10.

Counterparts. This Fourth Amendment may be executed in two (2) separate counterparts,
each of which shall be deemed an original, but all counterparts collectively shall constitute
one (1) instrument representing this Fourth Amendment between the parties. If any signature
is delivered by facsimile transmission or by email delivery of a “.pdf” format date file, such
signature shall create a valid and binding obligation of the party executing (or on whose behalf
such signature is executed) with the same force and effect as if such facsimile or “.pdf”
signature page were an original thereof.

11.

Successors and Assigns. This Fourth Amendment shall be binding upon, and shall inure
to the benefit of, the respective successors and assigns of the parties hereto.
