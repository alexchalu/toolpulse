About Living in Denver

Cost of Living Index: 115 (national average = 100). Denver is 15% more expensive than the US average.

Housing: The biggest expense in Denver. Average 1BR apartment costs $1,900/month.

Transportation: Good public transit system. Monthly pass costs $88.

Denver Living Costs Breakdown (2026)

  • Studio apartment: $1,500/month
  • 1 bedroom: $1,900/month
  • Groceries (1 person): $370/month
  • Transit pass: $88/month
  • Utilities: $115/month (electric, gas, water)

Tips for Living in Denver

  • Consider neighborhoods outside downtown for cheaper rent (20-40% savings)
  • Use public transit when possible - cheaper than car ownership
  • Cook at home - eating out costs 3-4x more than groceries
  • Look for roommates to split housing costs

📐 How We Calculate This

Our cost of living data draws from the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Consumer Price Index, the Council for Community and Economic Research (C2ER), and U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey. We index costs across housing, food, transportation, healthcare, and utilities.

Individual costs vary significantly based on lifestyle, household size, and specific neighborhood. These estimates represent metropolitan-area averages for typical households.
