🏥 Health Insurance in Virginia: What You Need to Know

Virginia uses the federally-facilitated marketplace (HealthCare.gov) for ACA-compliant individual and family health insurance plans. The state has expanded Medicaid, providing coverage to adults earning up to 138% of the Federal Poverty Level.

The largest health insurer in Virginia is Anthem Blue Cross Blue Shield. The average individual marketplace premium is approximately $5,880/year ($490/month) before any subsidies or tax credits.

With an uninsured rate of 7.8%, this is relatively close to or below the national average of ~8%.

💡 6 Ways to Lower Health Insurance Costs in Virginia

  • Check for premium subsidies: If your income is 100-400% FPL, you likely qualify for Premium Tax Credits that can reduce your monthly cost significantly.
  • Consider a Silver plan: Silver plans are the subsidy benchmark and offer Cost-Sharing Reductions (CSR) for incomes under 250% FPL, dramatically lowering deductibles and copays.
  • Use an HSA with a Bronze HDHP: If you're healthy, pair a Bronze High-Deductible Health Plan with a Health Savings Account for triple tax benefits.
  • Compare plans annually: Premiums and networks change every year. Always shop during Open Enrollment even if you're happy with your current plan.
  • Check if you qualify for Medicaid: With Medicaid expansion, adults earning up to $20,783/year for individuals qualify for free or very low-cost coverage.
  • Look for state-specific programs: Virginia may offer additional health coverage assistance programs, short-term health plans, or health care sharing ministries as alternatives.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much does health insurance cost in Virginia?
The average annual health insurance premium in Virginia is $5,880/year ($490/month) for an individual marketplace plan. Actual costs vary by age, tobacco use, location within Virginia, plan tier (Bronze/Silver/Gold/Platinum), and income-based subsidies.
Does Virginia have Medicaid expansion?
Yes, Virginia has expanded Medicaid under the ACA. Adults earning up to 138% of the Federal Poverty Level ($20,783 for an individual in 2026) qualify for Medicaid coverage.
What is the Open Enrollment period in Virginia?
Open Enrollment for Virginia's federal marketplace (HealthCare.gov) typically runs from November 1 through January 15. Some qualifying life events (job loss, marriage, birth of a child) allow Special Enrollment outside this window.
What is the uninsured rate in Virginia?
Virginia's uninsured rate is approximately 7.8%. The national average is approximately 8%.
Can I get subsidies for health insurance in Virginia?
If your household income is between 100-400% of the Federal Poverty Level ($14,580-$58,320 for an individual in 2026), you may qualify for Premium Tax Credits that lower your monthly premium. Enhanced subsidies under the Inflation Reduction Act cap premiums at 8.5% of income for all eligible enrollees.
Who is the largest health insurer in Virginia?
The largest health insurer in Virginia is Anthem Blue Cross Blue Shield. Multiple carriers compete in Virginia's marketplace, and plan availability varies by county and rating area.
