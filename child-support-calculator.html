Understanding Child Support Calculations

Income Shares Model: Used by most states. Both parents' incomes are combined, and support is calculated based on the proportion each parent would contribute if living together.

Percentage of Income Model: Child support is a fixed percentage of the non-custodial parent's income (varies by number of children).

Melson Formula: Ensures both parents retain enough income for basic needs before calculating support, then allocates remaining income.

Additional Expenses: Courts typically add healthcare and childcare costs on top of basic support obligations.

Custody Adjustments: Many states reduce support when the paying parent has significant parenting time (typically >30-40%).
