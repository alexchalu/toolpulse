About Child Support Calculations

Child support is a court-ordered payment from one parent to another to help cover the costs of raising a child. The amount is determined by state guidelines that consider:

  • Parental Income: Both parents' gross income from all sources
  • Number of Children: More children typically increase the total amount
  • Custody Time: How much time each parent spends with the child(ren)
  • Additional Expenses: Healthcare, childcare, education costs
  • State Guidelines: Each state uses different formulas and caps

Common Calculation Models:

  • Income Shares (Most States): Both parents contribute proportionally to what they would spend if living together
  • Percentage of Income: Fixed percentage of non-custodial parent's income (e.g., 17% for 1 child, 25% for 2)
  • Melson Formula: Ensures basic needs of parents before calculating support (DE, HI, MT)

Frequently Asked Questions

How is child support calculated?

Child support is calculated using state-specific guidelines based on parents' combined income, number of children, and custody arrangements. Most states use an "income shares" model that assumes both parents contribute proportionally.

What income is counted?

All sources of income are typically counted: wages, salaries, bonuses, commissions, self-employment income, rental income, investment income, unemployment benefits, and disability payments.

Can child support be modified?

Yes, child support can be modified if there's a significant change in circumstances, such as job loss, income increase, changes in custody, or changes in the child's needs. You must file a motion with the court.

How long does child support last?

Child support typically ends when the child turns 18 or graduates high school (whichever is later). In some states, it extends to age 21 or if the child has special needs.

What if I can't afford the payment?

Contact your state's child support agency immediately. Never stop paying without a court order. You may be able to request a modification based on changed circumstances.
