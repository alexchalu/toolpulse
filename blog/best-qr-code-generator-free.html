What Is a QR Code?

QR codes were invented by Denso Wave in 1994 for tracking automotive parts. Today they're everywhere — from restaurant menus to payment systems to marketing campaigns. A single QR code can store up to 4,296 characters of data.

Best Practices for QR Codes

  • Test before printing — always scan your QR code with multiple devices before mass-printing
  • Minimum size: 2 × 2 cm — smaller codes may not scan reliably
  • High contrast — dark code on light background works best; avoid low-contrast color combos
  • Add a call-to-action — "Scan to see menu" performs better than a bare code
  • Use URL shorteners — shorter URLs create simpler, more reliable QR codes
  • Don't put QR codes on screens people view on their phone — they can't scan what's on the screen they're holding

Frequently Asked Questions

Do QR codes expire?

Static QR codes never expire — the data is embedded directly in the image. Dynamic QR codes may expire if the hosting service shuts down or your subscription lapses.

Can I put a logo in a QR code?

Yes! QR codes have built-in error correction (up to 30% of the code can be damaged or obscured). This means you can place a small logo in the center and the code will still scan. Keep the logo under 20% of the total area for reliability.

Are QR codes secure?

QR codes themselves are just data carriers — they're as safe as the content they link to. Always preview the URL before opening it. Avoid scanning codes from untrusted sources, as they could link to phishing sites.
