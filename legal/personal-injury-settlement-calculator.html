How Personal Injury Settlements Are Calculated

Personal injury settlements typically consider several key factors:

  • Medical Expenses: All costs related to medical treatment, including future medical care
  • Lost Wages: Income lost due to inability to work
  • Pain and Suffering: Compensation for physical pain and emotional distress
  • Property Damage: Repair or replacement of damaged property
  • Punitive Damages: Additional compensation for egregious conduct (less common)
  • Loss of Consortium: Compensation for impact on relationships

Multipliers vs. Per Diem

Insurance companies often use two methods to calculate pain and suffering:

  • Multiplier Method: Medical bills × (1.5 to 5+) depending on injury severity
  • Per Diem Method: Daily rate × number of days affected

Comparative Negligence

In many states, if you share some fault for the accident, your compensation is reduced by your percentage of fault. This calculator accounts for comparative negligence.
