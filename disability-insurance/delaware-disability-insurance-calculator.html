💡 5 Tips to Save on Disability Insurance in Delaware

  1. Choose a longer elimination period. Going from 30 to 90 days can reduce premiums by 20-30%.
  2. Buy your own policy. Individual policies are portable and have "own occupation" definitions, unlike many employer plans.
  3. Buy young. Premiums are locked in at purchase age — buying at 30 vs 40 saves thousands over time.
  4. Consider a benefit period to age 65 for long-term protection vs a 5-year policy for lower premiums.
  5. Stack coverage. Combine employer group coverage with a supplemental individual policy to reach 60-70% income replacement.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much does disability insurance cost in Delaware?

In Delaware, private disability insurance typically costs 2.5% to 3.5% of the monthly benefit amount. For someone earning $54,690/year seeking 60% income replacement, expect to pay $82-$96/month depending on age, health, and occupation.

What is the average SSDI payment in Delaware?

The average SSDI monthly payment in Delaware is approximately $1,340, based on the state's average earnings history. The maximum federal SSDI benefit in 2026 is $3,822/month.

Does Delaware have state disability insurance?

No, Delaware does not have a state-mandated disability insurance program. Delaware residents must rely on private disability insurance, employer-sponsored coverage, or federal SSDI for income protection during disability.

How long does it take to get disability benefits in Delaware?

Private disability insurance typically begins paying after the elimination period (30-180 days). Federal SSDI has a mandatory 5-month waiting period, and the application process often takes 3-6 months.

What's the difference between short-term and long-term disability?

Short-term disability (STD) covers 3-6 months after a brief waiting period (0-14 days), replacing 60-70% of income. Long-term disability (LTD) kicks in after STD ends, covering years or until age 65 at 50-70% of income. Most Delaware workers need both for complete protection.

Is disability insurance tax-deductible in Delaware?

If you pay premiums with after-tax dollars (individual policy), your disability benefits are tax-free. If your employer pays premiums, benefits are taxable income. In Delaware, this applies to both federal and state taxes.
