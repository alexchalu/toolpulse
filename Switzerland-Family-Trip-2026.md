### Day 2 — Saturday, May 23: GRINDELWALD-FIRST ADVENTURE DAY

*The adventure mountain. Something for every age.*

**8:30 AM – Train to Grindelwald**
- Interlaken Ost → Grindelwald (35 minutes, included in Jungfrau Travel Pass)

**9:30 AM – Grindelwald-First Gondola**
- 25-minute gondola ride from Grindelwald village up to First (2,166m)
- Included in Jungfrau Travel Pass
- Kids love gondolas — scenic the whole way up

**10:00 AM – First Cliff Walk by Tissot**
- A suspended walkway along the cliff face at 2,166m
- Safe (enclosed railings), stroller-accessible, absolutely spectacular
- 20-minute walk with jaw-dropping views of Eiger, Mönch, Jungfrau
- FREE with gondola ticket
- ✅ Great for all ages including the 2-year-old in a stroller or carrier

**10:30 AM – First View Platform**
- Seven different vantage points looking out at the Bernese Alps
- ~20 minutes

**11:00 AM – Adventure Activities (for the bigger kids)**
These require separate tickets (~CHF 58 for adventure package):

| Activity | What It Is | Min. Height/Age | Good For |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| **First Flyer** | Zipline at 84 km/h over the Alps | Min. height ~130cm | 7-year-old (if tall enough) |
| **First Glider** | 4-person hang glider at 83 km/h | Min. height ~130cm | Same as above |
| **First Mountain Cart** | Go-kart on mountain road, Schreckfeld → Bort | Min. age ~6 | 5 & 7-year-olds, adults with younger kids |
| **First Trottibike** | Kick-scooter downhill, Bort → Grindelwald | Min. age ~6 | 5 & 7-year-olds |

> **For the younger kids (2 and 4):** Enjoy the **Bort Alpine Playground** — a 700-square-meter playground at the Bort middle station. Mountain streams to dam up, marmot and squirrel figures, rope nets, climbing structures. Spend 30-60 minutes here while older kids do adventure activities.

**12:30 PM – Lunch at First Mountain Restaurant**
- Self-service and à la carte options
- Outdoor terrace with panoramic views
- Swiss classics: bratwurst, rösti, cheese dishes

**2:00 PM – Option A: Hike to Lake Bachalpsee**
- Easy, well-maintained trail from First station
- ~50 minutes each way
- Destination: a stunning mirror-reflection Alpine lake at 2,265m
- The 2-year-old can ride in a carrier; 4-year-old can walk most of it
- **One of the most photographed spots in Switzerland**

**2:00 PM – Option B: Ride Down & Explore Grindelwald Village**
- Take gondola back down
- Walk through the charming village
- Ice cream, souvenir shops, cow bells
- More relaxed if kids are tired

**5:00 PM – Train back to Interlaken**

**Evening:** Dinner in Interlaken

### Day 3 — Sunday, May 24: LAUTERBRUNNEN VALLEY & WATERFALLS

*The fairy-tale valley. 72 waterfalls, sheer cliff walls, pure magic.*

**9:00 AM – Train to Lauterbrunnen**
- Interlaken Ost → Lauterbrunnen (20 minutes, included in Jungfrau Travel Pass)

**9:30 AM – Staubbach Falls**
- A 300-meter waterfall plunging right into the village
- Walk right up to it (short path from the village center)
- FREE
- Kids are mesmerized by the sheer scale
- You can walk behind the falls on a short trail (may be wet — bring rain jackets)
- ✅ Stroller-friendly access to the viewing area

**10:30 AM – Trümmelbach Falls**
- **10 glacial waterfalls INSIDE the mountain**
- Access via a tunnel lift (elevator inside the rock)
- 20,000 liters of water per second thundering through carved-out caves
- ~45-60 minute visit
- **Admission:** ~CHF 15/adult, children ~CHF 7, under 6 may be free
- ⚠️ It's LOUD inside — bring ear protection for the 2-year-old
- ⚠️ Can be slippery — hold hands with the little ones
- The 5 and 7-year-olds will think it's the coolest thing ever

**12:00 PM – Lunch in Lauterbrunnen village**
- Hotel Staubbach restaurant or Hotel Silberhorn
- Or pack a picnic from the supermarket and eat on a bench with waterfall views

**1:30 PM – Choose your afternoon:**

#### Option A: Boat Cruise on Lake Brienz + Ballenberg Open-Air Museum
- Train back to Interlaken, then boat from Interlaken Ost to Brienz (~75 min cruise)
- Boat included in Jungfrau Travel Pass!
- Beautiful turquoise lake — kids love boats

**Ballenberg Swiss Open-Air Museum** (in Brienz):
- **THE #1 family activity in Switzerland**
- 66 historic Swiss buildings from every region, relocated and rebuilt here
- Live farm animals — goats, cows, horses, chickens, donkeys (kids can pet them!)
- Watch blacksmiths, bakers, cheese-makers at work
- Bread-baking workshops
- New Ballenberg Train connects the whole grounds
- Plan 2-3 hours minimum
- **Open:** April 9 – November 1, 2026, daily 10 AM – 5 PM
- **Admission:** ~CHF 32/adult, children ~CHF 16, under 6 free
- **Website:** ballenberg.ch

#### Option B: Ride up to Wengen
- Cog railway from Lauterbrunnen to Wengen (15 min, included in JTP)
- Car-free mountain village — safe for kids to wander
- Walk through the village with panoramic Jungfrau views
- Playground, cafés, chocolate shops

**5:30 PM – Return to Interlaken**

**Evening:** Try **Swiss fondue** for dinner — melted cheese in a communal pot, everyone dips bread with long forks. The kids will love the dipping. Restaurant recommendations: ask your hotel or try Fondue-Stübli.

### Day 4 — Monday, May 25 (Memorial Day): JUNGFRAUJOCH — TOP OF EUROPE

*Europe's highest railway station. Snow in May. Once-in-a-lifetime.*

**⚠️ Important: Seat reservations are MANDATORY from May 1 – October 31.**
Book at jungfrau.ch — CHF 10 per person. Do this in advance!

**7:30 AM – Take the "Good Morning" Route (15% discount before 8:30 AM departure)**

**Route to the top:**
1. **Interlaken Ost → Grindelwald Terminal** (Bernese Oberland Railway, ~25 min)
2. **Grindelwald Terminal → Eigergletscher** (Eiger Express tricable gondola, ~15 min) — "the most modern tricable gondola in the world" with direct views of the famous Eiger North Face
3. **Eigergletscher → Jungfraujoch** (Jungfrau Railway through the mountain, ~25 min)

**Total journey: ~1 hour 30 minutes from Interlaken**

**Cost with Jungfrau Travel Pass:**
- Adults: CHF 89 each (connecting ticket — 50% off regular price)
- Children: CHF 30 each (or free with Swiss Family Card if under 6)
- **Total for 8 people: ~CHF 416 (~$466 USD)**

**At the Jungfraujoch (3,454m / 11,332 feet):**

| Attraction | Duration | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Sphinx Observation Terrace** | 15 min | Panoramic views — see as far as France & Germany. UNESCO World Heritage views of the Aletsch Glacier (longest in the Alps, 23 km) |
| **Glacier Plateau** | 15 min | Walk on snow — guaranteed 365 days a year. Kids can make snowballs in May! |
| **Ice Palace** | 15 min | Tunnels carved inside the glacier with ice sculptures of animals — eagles, penguins, bears. Kids love it. |
| **Snow Fun Park** | 45 min | Zipline over the glacier, snow tubing, sled rides. Check if open — may be weather-dependent. |
| **Alpine Sensation** | 15 min | Multimedia tunnel about the 100+ year history of the railway |
| **Lindt Swiss Chocolate Heaven** | 10 min | The highest Lindt chocolate shop in the world. Buy souvenirs! |
| **360° Cinema** | 5 min | Panoramic film of the mountain world |
| **Restaurants** | — | Aletsch Self (self-service, international), Crystal (à la carte Swiss), Bollywood (Indian!) |

**⚠️ CRITICAL: What to wear at the top**
- Temperature at Jungfraujoch in May: **-10°C to 0°C (14°F to 32°F)**
- **Everyone needs:** Winter jacket, warm hat, gloves, scarf, warm layers
- **The 2-year-old:** Snowsuit or heavy layers + warm carrier. Cover exposed skin.
- **Sunglasses:** Essential — the snow glare is intense
- **Sunscreen:** UV is very strong at this altitude

**⚠️ Altitude warning:**
- 3,454m is HIGH. Some people feel mild altitude effects (slight headache, breathlessness)
- Move slowly, drink water, don't overexert
- If any child seems unwell (very unusual for a short visit), descend immediately
- The visit is typically 2-3 hours, which is fine

**12:00 PM – Descend back to Grindelwald or Interlaken**

**Afternoon — Choose one:**

#### Option A: Funky Chocolate Club (Interlaken)
- Hands-on chocolate workshop
- Learn how Swiss chocolate is made
- Kids make their own chocolate creations
- Perfect wind-down activity after the mountain
- ~1.5 hours
- Book in advance at funkychocolateclub.com

#### Option B: Harder Kulm (Interlaken's own mountain)
- Funicular from Interlaken up to Harder Kulm (10 minutes, included in JTP)
- Observation deck with stunning views of BOTH lakes + Eiger/Mönch/Jungfrau
- Much gentler than Jungfraujoch — warm, easy, quick
- Restaurant at the top
- CHF 19/adult normally — **FREE with Jungfrau Travel Pass**

**Evening:** Special farewell dinner. Try a raclette restaurant — melted cheese scraped onto potatoes, pickles, and onions. Another Swiss classic that kids enjoy.

## 🎒 Part 7: Essential Tips & Practical Info

### Money & Currency
- **Currency:** Swiss Franc (CHF). 1 CHF ≈ $1.12 USD (check current rate)
- **Credit cards** accepted almost everywhere (Visa/Mastercard)
- **Tipping:** Not expected (service included), but rounding up is appreciated
- **ATMs:** Available at Zurich Airport and in Interlaken

### Language
- Region speaks **German** (Swiss German dialect)
- **English is widely spoken** in all tourist areas — no issues

### Power & Connectivity
- **Outlet type:** Type J (Swiss 3-pin). Bring a universal adapter.
- **Voltage:** 230V — same as most of Europe
- **Wi-Fi:** Available at all hotels; Swiss trains have Wi-Fi too
- **Cell service:** Check your carrier's international plan, or buy a Swiss SIM at the airport

### Safety
- Switzerland is one of the **safest countries in the world**
- Pickpocketing is extremely rare, even in tourist areas
- Tap water is safe and excellent
- Emergency number: **117** (police), **144** (ambulance), **118** (fire)

### Groceries & Dining
- **Coop** and **Migros** supermarkets are everywhere — great for snacks, sandwiches, drinks at 1/3 the restaurant price
- Restaurants are expensive (~CHF 25-40/adult main course, ~CHF 12-18/kids meal)
- **Kid-friendly Swiss foods:** Rösti, fondue, raclette, bratwurst, Zopf bread, Ovomaltine chocolate
- Tap water at restaurants is free if you ask for "Hahnenwasser" (tap water)

### For the Kids
- **Stroller:** Bring a lightweight, foldable stroller. Most paths and trains are accessible.
- **Baby carrier (backpack style):** Essential for the 2-year-old on mountain trails and at Jungfraujoch
- **Nap schedule:** Plan one big activity per morning, then flexible afternoons. Don't over-schedule.
- **Swiss trains have family compartments** with play areas on some routes — ask at the station
- **Diapers/wipes:** Available at Coop and Migros
