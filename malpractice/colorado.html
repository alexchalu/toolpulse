📋 Colorado Medical Malpractice Law Overview

Statute of Limitations: 2 years. In Colorado, you must file a medical malpractice claim within this period from when the injury was discovered or should have been discovered.

Damage Caps: $300K non-economic (adjustable). Colorado limits non-economic damages (pain & suffering), which may reduce your total recovery. Economic damages (medical bills, lost wages) are typically not capped.

Expert Requirements: Colorado requires a qualified medical expert to provide an affidavit or certificate of merit before filing a malpractice lawsuit. This expert must practice in the same or similar specialty as the defendant.

Modified Comparative Fault: In Colorado, your recovery may be reduced by your percentage of fault. If you are found more than 50% at fault, you may be barred from recovery entirely.

💡 5 Steps to Strengthen Your Colorado Malpractice Case

  1. Document Everything: Keep all medical records, bills, prescriptions, and correspondence. Request complete records from the healthcare provider immediately.
  2. Act Within the Deadline: Colorado's statute of limitations is 2 years. Missing this deadline permanently bars your claim regardless of merit.
  3. Get an Independent Medical Opinion: Have another qualified physician review your case. Colorado requires a certificate of merit from a medical expert.
  4. Consult a Colorado Malpractice Attorney: Most work on contingency (no win, no fee). Experienced attorneys know CO-specific procedures and damage cap implications.
  5. Don't Sign Anything: Insurance companies may pressure you into quick, low settlements. Never sign releases or give recorded statements without attorney guidance.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I have to file a medical malpractice lawsuit in Colorado?

In Colorado, the statute of limitations for medical malpractice is 2 years from the date of injury or discovery. Some exceptions apply for minors, fraud, or foreign objects left during surgery. Consult a Colorado attorney to understand your specific deadline.

How do damage caps affect my Colorado case?

Colorado caps non-economic damages at $300K non-economic (adjustable). This means pain and suffering awards are limited, but economic damages (medical bills, lost wages, future care costs) are typically not capped.

What is the average medical malpractice settlement in Colorado?

The average medical malpractice settlement in Colorado is approximately $510,000. However, settlements vary dramatically based on injury severity, from $50,000 for minor cases to several million for birth injuries or wrongful death.

How much does a Colorado malpractice attorney cost?

Most Colorado medical malpractice attorneys work on contingency, typically 33% of the settlement if resolved before trial and 40% if the case goes to trial. You pay nothing upfront, and if you don't win, you owe no attorney fees.

What do I need to prove in a Colorado malpractice case?

In Colorado, you must prove four elements: (1) a doctor-patient relationship existed, (2) the provider breached the standard of care, (3) that breach directly caused your injury, and (4) you suffered quantifiable damages. Expert medical testimony is required.

Can I sue a hospital in Colorado for malpractice?

Yes, hospitals in Colorado can be held liable for malpractice through vicarious liability (for employee doctors) or direct negligence (understaffing, equipment failures, credentialing failures). Government hospitals may have additional procedural requirements and shorter deadlines.
