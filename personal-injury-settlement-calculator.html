📊 How Personal Injury Settlements Are Calculated

Insurance companies primarily use two methods to calculate settlement offers:

Multiplier Method
Medical × 1.5–5

Total medical expenses multiplied by a severity factor, plus lost wages and other economic damages

Per Diem Method
Daily Rate × Days

A daily dollar amount (often your daily wage) for each day of pain and suffering from injury to maximum recovery

This calculator uses the multiplier method, which is the most commonly used approach by insurance adjusters and attorneys. The multiplier is based on injury severity:

Injury Level Multiplier Examples
Minor1.5×Soft tissue injuries, minor bruising, sprains
Mild2×Whiplash, minor fractures, concussions
Moderate3×Surgery needed, herniated disc, significant fractures
Severe4×Multiple surgeries, TBI, permanent limitations
Catastrophic5×Paralysis, amputation, permanent disability

Medical Documentation

Thorough medical records with consistent treatment significantly increase settlement value

📋
Liability Clarity

📜 Comparative Negligence by State Type

How your share of fault affects your settlement depends on your state's negligence law:

Pure Comparative
13 States

You can recover even if 99% at fault. Award reduced by your % of fault. (CA, NY, FL, etc.)

Modified 50% Bar
12 States

You recover if ≤49% at fault. At 50%+ you get nothing. (CO, GA, ME, etc.)

Modified 51% Bar
21 States

You recover if ≤50% at fault. At 51%+ you get nothing. (TX, OH, PA, IL, etc.)

Contributory
4 States + DC

If you're even 1% at fault, you get nothing. (AL, MD, NC, VA, DC)

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How is a personal injury settlement calculated?

Personal injury settlements are typically calculated using the multiplier method: total medical expenses × a severity factor (1.5–5×), plus lost wages, property damage, and other economic damages. Insurance adjusters also consider liability clarity, medical documentation quality, and policy limits.

What is the pain and suffering multiplier?

The pain and suffering multiplier ranges from 1.5× for minor injuries to 5× or more for catastrophic injuries. It's applied to your total medical bills to estimate non-economic damages like physical pain, emotional distress, loss of enjoyment of life, and inconvenience.

How long does a personal injury settlement take?

Most cases settle within 1–3 years. Simple fender-bender claims with clear liability may settle in months. Complex cases involving disputed fault, severe injuries, or multiple defendants can take 2–5+ years. Going to trial adds significant time and expense.

What percentage does a personal injury lawyer take?

Most personal injury attorneys work on contingency: 33.33% (one-third) for pre-trial settlements and 40% if the case goes to trial. You pay nothing upfront. Attorney fees, court costs, and medical liens are deducted from your settlement.

Should I accept the insurance company's first offer?

Almost never. Initial offers are typically 25–50% below fair value. Insurance adjusters are trained to minimize payouts. An attorney can negotiate a significantly higher settlement — studies show represented claimants receive 3.5× more on average than unrepresented ones.
